Project Overview

Where fashion
meets architecture

Located at the heart of Marisfrolg Fashion Group's Shenzhen campus, the Showroom interiors by Zaha Hadid Architects are informed by the rich textiles, timeless design, and precision tailoring embodied within the group's nine unique labels.

Featuring the main flagship store, a pop-up store for temporary exhibitions and events, a homeware gallery for décor and furniture, as well as a café and an outdoor courtyard, ZHA's interiors convey the ethos of Marisfrolg's unique labels.

Echoing the headquarters' original 'butterfly-wing' structure, ZHA's showroom design embodies a unifying architectural language of dynamism, with distinct elements unique to each label creating a different spatial experience to reflect their individuality.

Design Description

A unified language
of dynamism

The Butterfly-Wing Structure

Echoing the headquarters' original 'butterfly-wing' structure, ZHA's showroom design embodies a unifying architectural language of dynamism. Each label within the showroom is given a distinct spatial identity, creating different experiential zones that reflect the individuality of each brand while remaining visually cohesive within the whole.

"ZHA's interiors convey the ethos of Marisfrolg's unique labels — a material-rich gallery for the group."

Material Ethos

Crafted in recycled aggregates, the Marisfrolg Showroom becomes a material-rich gallery for the group. Its floors incorporate recycled crushed glass, quartz, sand, and stone, while its partition walls have been cast in glass reinforced gypsum (GRG) and fabricated from wax moulds that are recycled simply by melting and remilling the wax — an elegant solution that virtually eliminates mould waste.

Spatial Narrative

The flowing forms and organic geometries echo the fluidity of fabric in motion — each curve in the architecture mirrors the drape of a couture garment. The white surfaces function as both neutral backdrop and active architectural element, their topography catching light to create shadow volumes that shift throughout the day, animating the retail environment without relying on applied decoration.

Furniture & Displays

The showroom's ZHA-designed furniture and displays are composed in a variety of different recycled materials including glass, plastics, paper, fabrics, wood, and ceramics — each specifically designed to reflect and complement Marisfrolg's labels and products. Bespoke sculptural pieces, including the signature hanging installation, are designed as permanent art objects that anchor the spatial narrative.

Specification

Materials & Furniture

The Marisfrolg Showroom prioritises a circular material approach — every surface, partition, and furnishing has been specified with recyclability and longevity in mind. ZHA's design team collaborated directly with specialist fabricators to develop new applications for recycled and sustainable materials.

Recycled Aggregate Flooring
Floors incorporating recycled crushed glass, quartz, sand, and stone — creating a continuous terrazzo-like surface throughout the showroom.
Glass Reinforced Gypsum (GRG)
Partition walls cast in GRG using wax moulds that are fully recyclable by melting and remilling — eliminating mould waste from the construction process.
Bespoke Sculptural Furniture
ZHA-designed furniture and display units composed in recycled glass, plastics, paper, fabrics, wood, and ceramics — each calibrated to a specific label.
Polished White Resin Surfaces
Smooth white resin applied to walls, plinths, and display forms — providing a seamless, jointless surface that unifies the spatial language across all nine brand zones.
Full-Height Glass Partitions
Frameless glass panels used at key spatial transitions — maximising visual connectivity between zones while maintaining acoustic separation for VIP areas.
Textured Stone Paving
Herringbone-pattern stone paving used in transitional and entrance zones — referencing traditional craftsmanship while anchoring the contemporary interior language.

Physical Model

Campus Model

The Marisfrolg campus model, produced by Zaha Hadid Architects, reveals the full spatial ambition of the project — a masterplan of interlocking organic forms that respond to the existing butterfly-wing headquarters structure.

Individual showroom pods are articulated as distinct elliptical forms, connected by a network of flowing pathways that guide visitors through the campus. The model demonstrates the careful integration of landscape, water features, and built form that defines the campus planning strategy.
